A. Definitions

These definitions form part of Schedule 1 and should be read with the Scope of Inspection, Inspection Limitations and Terms and Conditions for the Timber Pest Inspection Service.

  • Client means the person or persons for whom the Inspection Report was carried out or their principal (i.e. the person or persons for whom the report is being obtained).
  • Consultant (also referred to as the Inspector) means a person, business or company who is qualified and experienced to undertake a Timber Pest Inspection Report. The Consultant must also meet any Government licensing requirement, where applicable. For this Agreement, the Consultant is SiteScop Pty Ltd or its authorised representative.
  • Building & Site means the inspection of the main residence and any attached structures within the agreed property boundary for timber pest purposes. A granny flat, studio or other secondary dwelling is not included in the standard main-residence fee unless specifically agreed and paid for as an additional fee item.
  • Readily Accessible Areas means areas which can be easily and safely inspected without injury to person or property, are not affected by inspection limitations, or where clearances are not available, areas within the Consultant's unobstructed line of sight and within arm's length.
  • Major Defect means a defect of significant magnitude where rectification must be carried out to avoid unsafe conditions, loss of utility or further deterioration of the property.
  • Minor Defect means a defect other than a Major Defect.
  • Serious Safety Hazard means any item that may constitute an immediate or imminent risk to health or life.
  • Obstruction means any condition or physical limitation which inhibits or prevents inspection and may include (but is not limited to) roofing, fixed ceilings, wall linings, floor coverings, fixtures, fittings, furniture, clothes, stored articles/materials, thermal insulation, sarking, pipe/duct work, builders' debris, vegetation, pavements or earth.
  • Limitation means any areas which are inaccessible, not readily accessible or obstructed at the time of inspection.
  • Confined Space means an enclosed or partially enclosed area that is not designed for continuous occupancy and where entry, exit, clearance or atmosphere may present a safety risk. The Inspector will not enter confined spaces (or areas that may constitute confined spaces) where safe access and egress, adequate clearance, or a safe atmosphere cannot be confirmed.
Note: Evidence of timber pest activity or damage is assessed under the Timber Pest Inspection scope. A finding is only also a Serious Safety Hazard where it constitutes an immediate or imminent risk to health or life.

Scope of Timber Pest Inspection

This Timber Pest Inspection has been requested by the Client and will be carried out by SiteScop Pty Ltd as a visual inspection for evidence of timber pest activity, damage and conditions conducive to timber pest attack. The inspection is limited to the readily accessible areas of the property and the conditions visible at the time of the inspection.

1. Purpose of Inspection

The purpose of the inspection is to identify visible evidence of timber pests, including termites, borers and wood decay fungi, together with visible conditions that may increase the risk of timber pest attack. The inspection is intended to assist the Client in making an informed decision regarding the condition of the property but does not guarantee that timber pests are absent.

The inspection is visual only and does not include destructive testing or dismantling of building components unless specifically agreed in writing.

2. Scope of Inspection

The Timber Pest Inspection is a non-invasive visual inspection of the readily accessible areas of the property for evidence of timber pest activity, damage and conditions conducive to timber pest attack. The inspection is carried out without removing floor coverings, wall linings, ceiling linings, insulation, furniture, stored goods or any other building component.

Only areas that are safe and reasonably accessible at the time of inspection can be inspected.

3. Timber Pests Covered

The inspection includes visible evidence of:

  • Subterranean termites.
  • Dampwood termites.
  • Drywood termites (where applicable).
  • Wood borers.
  • Wood decay fungi.
  • Visible termite workings.
  • Visible termite damage.
  • Previous termite treatments.
  • Previous termite damage.
  • Conditions conducive to timber pest attack.

4. Areas Inspected

Where safe and readily accessible, the inspection may include:

  • External perimeter.
  • Internal living areas.
  • Roof void.
  • Subfloor.
  • Garage.
  • Carport.
  • Pergolas.
  • Decks.
  • Fences attached to the dwelling.
  • Retaining walls adjacent to the dwelling.
  • Timber structures.
  • Garden areas immediately surrounding the building.

5. Conditions Conducive to Timber Pest Attack

The Inspector will identify visible conditions that may increase the likelihood of timber pest attack including:

  • Excessive ground moisture.
  • Leaking plumbing.
  • Poor drainage.
  • Timber in contact with soil.
  • Inadequate ventilation.
  • High garden beds.
  • Bridged termite management systems.
  • Cellulose debris.
  • Stored timber against the building.
  • Excessive vegetation.
The absence of visible timber pest activity does not guarantee that timber pests are not present within concealed areas of the property.

6. Inspection Method

The Timber Pest Inspection is conducted using a non-invasive visual examination of the readily accessible areas of the property. The Inspector may use standard inspection equipment including:

  • High-powered inspection torch.
  • Moisture meter.
  • Thermal imaging camera (where applicable).
  • Sounding tool.
  • Probe or donger.
  • Mirror.
  • Digital camera.
  • Ladder where safe and practical.

These tools assist the Inspector in identifying visible evidence of timber pest activity but cannot detect concealed infestations in every circumstance.

7. Timber Pest Evidence

The inspection includes reporting of visible evidence such as:

  • Live termites.
  • Dead termites.
  • Termite mud shelter tubes.
  • Damaged timber.
  • Hollow sounding timber.
  • Exit holes from timber borers.
  • Borer frass.
  • Wood decay fungi.
  • Previous termite treatment evidence.
  • Previous termite damage.

8. Timber Pest Management Systems

Where visible, the Inspector may report on the apparent presence and general condition of termite management systems including:

  • Chemical management systems.
  • Physical barriers.
  • Stainless steel mesh barriers.
  • Graded stone barriers.
  • Inspection zones.
  • Visible breaches or bridging.

The inspection does not certify the effectiveness, serviceability or ongoing performance of any termite management system.

9. Moisture and Environmental Conditions

Excess moisture is one of the primary factors contributing to termite activity. Where visible evidence of excessive moisture, plumbing leaks, poor drainage or ventilation issues is observed, these conditions may be reported because they increase the risk of future timber pest attack.

Conditions conducive to timber pest attack should be corrected promptly to reduce the likelihood of future infestation.

10. Items Excluded

Unless specifically agreed in writing, this Timber Pest Inspection does not include:

  • Destructive testing.
  • Opening wall, ceiling or floor cavities.
  • Removing insulation.
  • Removing floor coverings.
  • Moving furniture or stored goods.
  • Excavation of soil.
  • Inspection below concrete slabs.
  • Inspection of inaccessible roof spaces.
  • Inspection of inaccessible subfloors.
  • Assessment of structural adequacy.
  • Building code compliance.
  • Electrical, plumbing or gas systems.
  • Swimming pools and spas.
  • Trees, landscaping or detached timber structures unless specifically included.
Timber pests may be present within concealed areas that cannot be inspected during a visual inspection. No visual inspection can guarantee that a property is free from timber pests.

11. Recommendations

Where evidence of active timber pests, previous damage, conditions conducive to attack or deficiencies in termite management systems are identified, the Inspector may recommend further investigation or remedial action. Recommendations may include:

  • Licensed termite treatment.
  • Further invasive inspection.
  • Repair of plumbing leaks.
  • Improvement of site drainage.
  • Removal of timber-to-soil contact.
  • Improvement of subfloor ventilation.
  • Removal of cellulose debris.
  • Regular timber pest inspections.

12. Ongoing Timber Pest Management

Timber pest inspections provide information about the visible condition of the property only at the time of inspection. Properties located in Australia remain at risk of future timber pest attack regardless of previous inspection results. SiteScop Pty Ltd recommends that timber pest inspections be carried out at intervals not exceeding 12 months, or more frequently where recommended by the Inspector or where local conditions increase the risk of infestation.

13. Client Acknowledgement

By proceeding with this Timber Pest Inspection, the Client acknowledges that they understand:

  • The inspection is visual only.
  • Hidden infestations may exist.
  • Inaccessible areas cannot be inspected.
  • The inspection does not guarantee the absence of timber pests.
  • Recommendations should be acted upon promptly.

14. Applicable Standard

This Timber Pest Inspection has been carried out generally in accordance with Australian Standard AS 4349.3 – Inspection of Buildings – Timber Pest Inspections, except where otherwise agreed in writing with the Client.

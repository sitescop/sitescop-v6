Timber Pest Inspection Limitations

This document explains the limitations that apply to the Timber Pest Inspection and Timber Pest Inspection Report. The inspection is a non-invasive visual inspection only and is limited by the accessibility, visibility and condition of the property at the time of inspection.

1. Visual Inspection Only

The inspection is limited to the visible and readily accessible areas of the property. No destructive testing is carried out. The Inspector will not remove wall linings, floor coverings, ceiling linings, insulation, stored goods, furniture or other building components to search for concealed timber pests.

Hidden termite activity, concealed timber damage and timber pests within inaccessible areas may remain undetected.

2. Readily Accessible Areas

Only areas that can be safely accessed without risk of injury or damage to the property will be inspected. Areas may be inaccessible due to:

  • Locked rooms.
  • Stored goods.
  • Furniture.
  • Wall linings.
  • Ceiling linings.
  • Insulation.
  • Restricted roof access.
  • Restricted subfloor clearance.
  • Unsafe conditions.
  • Building design.
Areas that are inaccessible cannot be inspected and therefore cannot be reported upon.

3. Roof Spaces

Roof spaces are inspected only where safe and practical access is available. The Inspector will not enter roof spaces where access is unsafe or where there is a risk of injury or damage to the property. Inspection may be restricted by:

  • Limited access openings.
  • Low roof clearance.
  • Excessive insulation.
  • Unsafe framing.
  • Electrical hazards.
  • High temperatures.
  • Fragile ceiling linings.

4. Subfloor Areas

Subfloor inspections are limited to areas that are safely accessible. Inspection may be restricted by:

  • Insufficient clearance.
  • Standing water.
  • Stored materials.
  • Unsafe structural conditions.
  • Electrical hazards.
  • Animal activity.
  • Excessive debris.
Where access to roof spaces or subfloor areas is restricted, active termite infestations or timber damage may remain undetected.

5. Concealed Timber Pest Activity

Timber pests frequently remain concealed within wall cavities, floor framing, roof framing and other enclosed building elements. The inspection cannot determine whether concealed activity is present where direct visual evidence is unavailable. No visual inspection can locate every infestation.

6. Previous Timber Pest Treatments

Where evidence of previous termite treatment or termite management systems is visible, the Inspector may comment on its apparent presence. The inspection does not verify:

  • The effectiveness of previous treatments.
  • The remaining life of chemical barriers.
  • The condition of concealed physical barriers.
  • Warranty coverage.
  • Compliance with installation requirements.

7. Weather Conditions

Weather conditions before or during the inspection may affect the ability to identify timber pest activity. Heavy rain, recent rainfall, excessive moisture, poor lighting or extreme temperatures may reduce the effectiveness of the inspection. Timber pests may also alter their activity according to seasonal and environmental conditions.

8. Occupied Properties

Where the property is occupied, furniture, stored goods, personal belongings and floor coverings may prevent complete inspection of all building components. The Inspector is not required to move furniture, appliances, stored items or personal possessions. Areas concealed by such items are excluded from the inspection.

The Client should ensure the property is as accessible as possible before the inspection to maximise the effectiveness of the inspection.

9. Timber Pest Management Systems

Where termite management systems are visible, the Inspector may comment on their apparent presence and general condition. The inspection does not include certification of the design, installation, effectiveness or remaining service life of any termite management system.

Examples include:

  • Chemical barriers.
  • Physical barriers.
  • Stainless steel mesh systems.
  • Graded stone systems.
  • Inspection zones.
  • Reticulation systems.
  • Termite baiting systems.
The existence of a termite management system does not guarantee protection against future termite attack. Regular inspections remain essential.

10. Timber Damage Assessment

The inspection identifies only visible timber damage. The Inspector does not determine the structural adequacy, engineering performance or remaining strength of damaged timber. Where significant timber damage is identified, further assessment by a suitably qualified building professional or structural engineer may be recommended.

11. Moisture Conditions

Moisture readings obtained during the inspection are intended only as an aid to identifying areas that may warrant further investigation. Moisture meters cannot determine:

  • The source of moisture.
  • The duration of moisture exposure.
  • Hidden plumbing leaks.
  • Future moisture ingress.
  • Whether timber decay has commenced.

12. Environmental Factors

The inspection does not include assessment of environmental conditions including:

  • Asbestos-containing materials.
  • Lead-based paint.
  • Mould identification or laboratory testing.
  • Air quality.
  • Hazardous substances.
  • Soil contamination.
  • Ground stability.
Separate specialist inspections should be obtained where these issues are of concern to the Client.

13. Future Infestation

The Timber Pest Inspection Report represents the condition of the property only at the date and time of inspection. Timber pest activity may commence or become visible after the inspection due to changes in environmental conditions, building maintenance, moisture levels or other factors beyond the Inspector's control. No inspection can predict future timber pest activity.

14. Recommendations

Where evidence of active timber pests, previous timber damage, conditions conducive to timber pest attack or deficiencies in existing termite management systems are identified, the Inspector may recommend further investigation or remedial action. Recommendations may include:

  • Immediate treatment by a licensed pest management professional.
  • Further invasive inspection.
  • Repair of plumbing leaks.
  • Improvement of site drainage.
  • Removal of timber-to-soil contact.
  • Removal of cellulose debris.
  • Improvement of subfloor ventilation.
  • Ongoing annual timber pest inspections.

15. Client Acknowledgement

By proceeding with the Timber Pest Inspection, the Client acknowledges and accepts that:

  • The inspection is limited to a visual inspection of the readily accessible areas.
  • Hidden timber pest activity may remain undetected.
  • Inaccessible areas cannot be inspected.
  • No guarantee is given that the property is free from timber pests.
  • Future timber pest attack may occur after the inspection.
  • Recommendations contained within the Inspection Report should be acted upon promptly.
Failure to address active timber pest infestations or conditions conducive to timber pest attack may result in significant structural damage and costly repairs.

16. Applicable Standard

This Timber Pest Inspection has been undertaken generally in accordance with Australian Standard AS 4349.3 – Inspection of Buildings – Timber Pest Inspections, except where otherwise agreed in writing between the Client and SiteScop Pty Ltd.

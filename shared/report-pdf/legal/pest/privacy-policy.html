Privacy Policy

SiteScop Pty Ltd is committed to protecting the privacy of our clients and handling personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). This Privacy Policy explains how personal information is collected, used, stored and disclosed when providing Timber Pest Inspection services.

1. Information We Collect

Depending on the services requested, we may collect:

  • Client name.
  • Postal address.
  • Inspection property address.
  • Email address.
  • Telephone and mobile numbers.
  • Inspection booking information.
  • Inspection reports.
  • Photographs and thermal images.
  • Electronic signatures.
  • Communication records.
  • Payment information (where applicable).

2. How Information is Collected

Personal information may be collected through:

  • Inspection booking forms.
  • Our website.
  • Email correspondence.
  • Telephone conversations.
  • SMS communications.
  • Inspection Agreement forms.
  • Inspection reports.
  • Face-to-face meetings.
Only information reasonably necessary to provide our services is collected.

3. How We Use Your Information

SiteScop Pty Ltd collects and uses personal information only for purposes reasonably necessary to provide our inspection services. Your information may be used to:

  • Arrange inspection appointments.
  • Conduct Timber Pest Inspections.
  • Prepare and deliver Inspection Reports.
  • Communicate regarding bookings and inspections.
  • Issue invoices and receive payments.
  • Respond to enquiries and customer support requests.
  • Meet legal and regulatory obligations.
  • Improve our services and internal systems.

4. Disclosure of Personal Information

SiteScop Pty Ltd does not sell or rent personal information to third parties. Information may be disclosed where reasonably necessary to:

  • Professional advisers.
  • Cloud storage providers.
  • Email and communication providers.
  • Payment service providers.
  • Government authorities where required by law.
  • Courts or regulatory bodies where legally required.
Personal information will only be disclosed where permitted or required by Australian law.

5. Storage and Security

Reasonable administrative, technical and physical safeguards are used to protect personal information against loss, unauthorised access, misuse, disclosure or alteration. Personal information may be stored electronically using secure systems and cloud services. Access is restricted to authorised personnel who require the information to perform their duties.

6. Retention of Information

Inspection records, reports and related documentation may be retained for as long as reasonably necessary to satisfy business, insurance and legal obligations. When information is no longer required, reasonable steps will be taken to securely destroy or de-identify it.

7. Access and Correction

Clients may request access to personal information held by SiteScop Pty Ltd and may request correction of inaccurate, incomplete or outdated information, subject to applicable Australian law.

8. Website and Cookies

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to improve functionality, analyse website usage and enhance the user experience. Users may disable cookies through their browser settings, although some website features may not function correctly.

9. Changes to this Privacy Policy

SiteScop Pty Ltd may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The latest version published forms the current Privacy Policy.

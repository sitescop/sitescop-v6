Scope of Building Inspection

This Building Inspection has been requested by the Client and will be completed by SiteScop in accordance with Australian Standard AS 4349.1 – Inspection of Buildings – Pre-Purchase Inspections – Residential Buildings unless otherwise agreed in writing.

1. Purpose of Inspection

The purpose of this inspection is to provide the Client with an independent visual assessment of the condition of the building at the date and time of inspection. The inspection is intended to identify significant defects, major defects, safety hazards and conditions requiring further investigation.

1. Purpose of Inspection

The purpose of this inspection is to provide the Client with an independent visual assessment of the condition of the building at the date and time of inspection. The inspection is intended to identify significant defects, major defects, safety hazards and conditions requiring further investigation.

2. Scope of Inspection

The inspection is a non-invasive visual assessment of the readily accessible areas of the property. The inspection does not include dismantling, removing, lifting, cutting, excavating, testing, drilling or otherwise damaging any part of the building. Only areas that were safe and reasonably accessible at the time of inspection are included.

Readily Accessible

A readily accessible area is an area that can be safely inspected without the use of destructive methods, specialist equipment or unreasonable risk.

3. Areas Included

Where safely accessible, the inspection may include the following components:

  • Site and surrounding grounds
  • Foundations
  • Footings (visible only)
  • External walls
  • Internal walls
  • Floors
  • Ceilings
  • Roof exterior
  • Roof space
  • Subfloor
  • Windows
  • External doors
  • Internal doors
  • Garage
  • Carport
  • Balconies
  • Verandahs
  • Pergolas
  • Retaining walls (where applicable)
  • Paths and driveways
  • Drainage observations
  • Moisture observations
  • Visible structural elements

4. Areas Excluded

The inspection does not include any area that was:

  • Locked
  • Inaccessible
  • Unsafe
  • Obstructed
  • Concealed by furniture, storage or finishes
  • Covered by insulation
  • Below ground level
  • Inside wall cavities
  • Inside ceilings
  • Inside floor cavities not accessible
  • Services concealed within the building
  • Areas requiring destructive inspection

5. Defects Assessed During the Inspection

Major Defects

A major defect is a defect that requires significant repair, replacement or further investigation. Major defects may affect the structural integrity, safety, serviceability or durability of the building.

Minor Defects

Minor defects generally include normal wear and tear, maintenance items, cosmetic deterioration and minor damage that does not significantly affect the performance of the building.

Safety Hazards

The inspection includes visible safety hazards observed at the time of inspection. Examples include loose balustrades, trip hazards, damaged stairs, exposed electrical components, unsafe handrails and other visible hazards.

Structural Observations

The inspection includes visual observations of structural elements including movement, cracking, settlement, deformation, deterioration and other visible signs that may indicate structural concerns. The inspection is not a structural engineering assessment.

Moisture Observations

Where moisture staining or evidence of water ingress is observed, the inspector may use a non-invasive moisture meter to assist with the assessment. Moisture readings are indicative only and do not identify the source or cause of moisture.

6. Inspection Method

The inspection is completed using a visual examination of accessible building elements together with professional judgement, experience and standard inspection tools. Equipment may include:

  • High-powered inspection torch
  • Moisture meter
  • Thermal imaging camera (where applicable)
  • Digital camera
  • Ladder (where safe and practical)
  • Measuring devices

No destructive testing is undertaken unless separately agreed in writing.

Important

The inspection represents the condition of the property only at the time of inspection. Future deterioration, movement, weather events or changes to the property are outside the scope of this inspection.

7. Items Specifically Excluded

Unless specifically agreed in writing, this inspection does not include assessment of:

  • Hidden or concealed defects.
  • Footings below ground level.
  • Underground drainage systems.
  • Electrical installations.
  • Gas installations.
  • Plumbing systems.
  • Air conditioning systems.
  • Swimming pools and spas.
  • Solar systems.
  • Security systems.
  • Appliances and equipment.
  • Building compliance or certification.
  • Environmental hazards.
  • Asbestos identification.
  • Lead paint.
  • Mould testing.
  • Geotechnical conditions.
  • Boundary surveys.
  • Valuation of the property.
Important

The absence of reported defects does not guarantee that defects do not exist. Concealed defects may be present that cannot be detected during a visual inspection.

8. Recommendations

Where evidence of significant defects, deterioration, structural movement or conditions requiring specialist assessment is observed, the Inspector may recommend further investigation by an appropriately qualified specialist before the property is purchased or occupied. Recommendations may include consultation with:

  • Structural Engineer
  • Licensed Electrician
  • Licensed Plumber
  • Roofing Contractor
  • Waterproofing Specialist
  • Drainage Specialist
  • Geotechnical Engineer
  • Other suitably qualified trades or consultants

9. Client Acknowledgement

By proceeding with this inspection, the Client acknowledges that they have read and understood the Scope of Inspection and accept that the inspection is limited to a visual assessment of the readily accessible areas of the property at the time of inspection. The Client further acknowledges that no warranty or guarantee is provided that every defect or condition will be identified.

10. Applicable Standard

This inspection has been prepared generally in accordance with Australian Standard AS 4349.1 – Inspection of Buildings – Pre-Purchase Inspections – Residential Buildings, except where otherwise agreed in writing with the Client.

End of Scope of Building Inspection

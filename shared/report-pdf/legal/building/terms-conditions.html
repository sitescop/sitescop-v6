1. Definitions

For the purposes of this Agreement:

  • Inspector means SiteScop Pty Ltd or its authorised representative carrying out the inspection.
  • Client means the person or entity requesting the inspection.
  • Property means the building or buildings nominated by the Client.

  • Report means the written Building Inspection Report provided following the inspection.

2. Inspection Agreement

The Client engages SiteScop Pty Ltd to undertake a visual Building Inspection of the nominated property. The inspection will generally be completed in accordance with Australian Standard AS 4349.1 – Inspection of Buildings – Pre-Purchase Inspections – Residential Buildings unless otherwise agreed in writing.

The inspection is a visual assessment only and does not constitute a guarantee, certification or warranty regarding the condition of the property.

3. Inspection Service

The Inspector will exercise reasonable care and professional skill when conducting the inspection. The inspection is limited to the readily accessible areas of the property and to conditions visible at the time of the inspection. No destructive testing or dismantling of building elements will be performed unless separately agreed in writing.

  • The inspection scope is changed after booking.

  • Return visits are required due to restricted access.

  • Specialist inspections are requested.

The Client is responsible for ensuring that reasonable access to the property is available at the agreed inspection time. Additional fees may apply where a further inspection is required because access was unavailable.

6. Client Responsibilities

The Client acknowledges and agrees to:

  • Seek specialist advice where recommended.
  • Not rely upon verbal comments in place of the written report.
The written Inspection Report forms the complete professional opinion of the Inspector. Verbal comments are general in nature and must not be relied upon independently of the written report.

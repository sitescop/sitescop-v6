Building Inspection Limitations

This document explains the limitations that apply to the Building Inspection and Inspection Report. The inspection is a visual assessment only and is limited by the accessibility, visibility and condition of the property at the time of inspection.

1. Visual Inspection Only

The inspection is limited to visible and readily accessible areas. No destructive testing is undertaken. The Inspector will not remove wall linings, floor coverings, ceiling linings, insulation, furniture, stored goods, landscaping or finishes to inspect concealed components.

Hidden defects may exist that cannot reasonably be identified during a visual inspection.

2. Readily Accessible Areas

The inspection is limited to areas that were safe and readily accessible at the time of inspection. Areas may be inaccessible due to:

  • Locked rooms
  • Stored goods
  • Furniture
  • Floor coverings
  • Wall linings
  • Ceiling linings
  • Insulation
  • Roof pitch
  • Limited roof access
  • Limited subfloor clearance
  • Safety risks
  • Building design

Items concealed behind finishes or construction materials cannot be inspected and are therefore excluded from the scope of the inspection.

3. Roof Spaces

Roof spaces are inspected only where safe access is available. The Inspector will not enter roof spaces where access is unsafe or where there is a risk of injury or property damage. Examples include:

  • Unsafe framing
  • Excessive insulation
  • Restricted access openings
  • Electrical hazards
  • Excessive temperatures
  • Insufficient clearance

4. Subfloor Areas

Subfloor areas are inspected only where safe and practical. Inspection may be restricted where there is:

  • Insufficient clearance
  • Standing water
  • Stored materials
  • Electrical hazards
  • Animal activity
  • Unsafe structural conditions
Where access restrictions exist, defects may remain concealed and cannot be reported.

5. Weather Conditions

Weather conditions at the time of inspection may influence the ability to identify defects. Rain, recent rainfall, excessive moisture, wind or poor lighting conditions may reduce the effectiveness of the inspection. Some defects may only become apparent under different weather conditions or after prolonged occupation.

6. Concealed Defects

The inspection cannot identify defects that are concealed by building materials, finishes, fixtures, furniture, stored items, vegetation or any other obstruction. Examples include:

  • Hidden structural damage.
  • Defective framing concealed within walls.
  • Concealed moisture ingress.
  • Leaks within wall cavities.
  • Concealed plumbing defects.
  • Hidden electrical defects.
  • Insulation-covered defects.
  • Subsurface movement.
The absence of reported defects does not indicate that hidden or concealed defects do not exist.

7. Occupied Properties

Where the property is occupied, inspection may be restricted by furniture, stored belongings, floor coverings, personal items and limited access provided by the occupant. The Inspector is not required to move furniture, appliances, stored goods or personal belongings. Areas that cannot be inspected due to these restrictions are excluded from the inspection.

8. Stored Goods and Obstructions

Inspection cannot be carried out where building components are obstructed by stored goods, shelving, heavy furniture, appliances, vegetation or other materials. The Inspector accepts no responsibility for defects concealed by such obstructions.

9. Services Excluded

Unless specifically agreed in writing, the following services are excluded from the inspection:

  • Electrical installation testing.
  • Gas installation testing.
  • Plumbing system testing.
  • Hot water system performance.
  • Air conditioning systems.
  • Security systems.
  • Solar systems.
  • Communications systems.
  • Underground services.
Separate specialist inspections should be obtained where the condition or performance of these services is important to the Client.

10. Environmental Conditions

The inspection does not include assessment of environmental conditions or contaminants unless specifically agreed in writing. Examples include:

  • Asbestos-containing materials.
  • Lead-based paint.
  • Mould identification or laboratory testing.
  • Air quality.
  • Soil contamination.
  • Hazardous chemicals.
  • Radon gas.
  • Underground contamination.

11. Future Performance

The inspection represents the condition of the property only at the date and time of inspection. No guarantee or warranty is provided regarding the future performance, deterioration or serviceability of any building component. Defects may develop after the inspection due to weather, ground movement, age, normal wear and tear, maintenance, building alterations or other causes beyond the Inspector's control.

12. Further Investigation

Where visible evidence indicates possible structural issues, water ingress, movement or other significant concerns, the Inspector may recommend further investigation by an appropriately qualified specialist. Such recommendations should be completed before exchanging contracts or proceeding with the purchase wherever practical.

Failure to obtain recommended specialist advice may expose the Client to defects, repair costs or risks that were outside the scope of this visual inspection.

13. Client Acknowledgement

By proceeding with the inspection, the Client acknowledges that they have read and understood these Inspection Limitations and accept that the inspection is restricted to a visual assessment of the readily accessible areas of the property. The Client understands that concealed defects, inaccessible areas and excluded items cannot be reported upon.

14. Applicable Standard

This Building Inspection has been undertaken generally in accordance with Australian Standard AS 4349.1 – Inspection of Buildings – Pre-Purchase Inspections – Residential Buildings, except where otherwise agreed in writing.

End of Building Inspection Limitations
